Hospitality Types

Built for the way
your venue runs

Hospitality isn't one business — it's many. A quick service counter, a stadium concourse, and a multi-site restaurant group all have different rhythms, different pressures, and different definitions of a good day. QJumper is shaped to fit each of them.

One platform, many operating styles

Most hospitality tools are designed for a single context — a café, a restaurant, a fast-food chain — and everything else is bent to fit. That's how venues end up disabling features, overriding workflows, and paying for things they'll never use.

QJumper flexes to each venue

Same platform. Different shape for each operator.

A franchise group runs QJumper differently from a stadium, and a stadium runs it differently from a neighbourhood café. The underlying platform is the same — the surfaces, settings, and emphasis are tuned to how the venue actually works.

Venue 01

Multi-site & franchise restaurants

When the same menu is served across ten, twenty, or fifty sites, consistency becomes everything. QJumper gives head office a single source of truth — menus, pricing, promotions, and rules managed centrally and pushed to every location, while each site still has the freedom to run its own service.

  • Central menu and pricing control across every location
  • Site-level overrides for local specials, hours, and stock
  • Cross-site reporting that reconciles automatically
  • Franchisee access that's scoped to their own venues
  • Staged rollouts of new menu items or promotions
Venue 02

Quick service venues

In quick service, every second matters. QJumper is tuned for throughput — kiosks that move a queue quickly, a POS that cashiers master in a shift, and kitchen workflows that keep orders flowing when the lunch rush hits hardest.

  • Order-taking that's quicker than a queue at the counter
  • Kiosks sized and flowed for high-throughput service
  • Upsell prompts that lift average order value without slowing service
  • Kitchen displays that route orders by station automatically
  • Live wait-time and throughput metrics on every screen
Venue 03

Stadiums & events

Stadium hospitality is a series of sudden, massive peaks — halftime, the interval, doors opening. QJumper is architected for those bursts, with in-seat ordering, queue-busting kiosks, and concourse payments that don't buckle when 20,000 people want service at once.

  • In-seat mobile and web ordering with bay runner routing
  • Concourse kiosks and counter POS sharing the same queue
  • Pre-order and click-and-collect ahead of kick-off
  • Event-mode pricing and menus that switch per fixture
  • Throughput dashboards for ops leads on the night
Venue 04

Cafés & single-site restaurants

A neighbourhood café doesn't need an enterprise rollout — it needs a platform that's easy to open, quick to learn, and grows with the business. QJumper starts simple, and the deeper features are there when you're ready for them.

  • Fast, lightweight setup with sensible defaults
  • Table ordering via QR with no app download
  • Branded mobile app and web ordering without custom build
  • Reports that make sense to an owner-operator, not just an analyst
  • A clear path to multi-site when the business grows
Other venues

Hotels, bars, convention centres & more

If you run a venue that serves food or drink at pace — a hotel F&B operation, a bar group, a convention centre, a festival, a ski-field café — QJumper is almost certainly a fit. The platform was designed to be flexed, not rewritten, for different operating styles.

  • Hotel F&B — room service, pool bar, in-house restaurants on one platform
  • Bars & nightlife — rapid tab management, multi-bartender workflows
  • Convention centres & conferences — delegate pre-order and pickup
  • Festivals & outdoor events — offline-tolerant, cashless concessions
  • Cinemas & attractions — concessions tied to ticketing
